About Me

♪ ♪ Getting to Know You...♪♪

I was born and raised in Baltimore, Maryland. Upon graduation from high school, I moved to Alabama to attend Tuskegee University as a pre-med biology major. It was there that I joined Air Force ROTC becoming completely immersed in aviation. Deciding that I would pursue a civillian career in aviation, I returned to Maryland to study aviation management. It was then that I began flying small single-engine aircraft. I loved every minute of it! After nearly a decade of working in aviation, I am called to tech.

My first taste of coding came when I randomly decided to build an air traffic control simulator to help my peers study for the AT-SAT (a standardized test administered to air traffic control students to access their job qualification level). There weren't any practice simulators available so I challenged myself to build one. With absolutely no experience or prior coding knowledge, I completed the simulator in just 30 days using canvas, HTML, CSS and JavaScript. Busy with my aviaiton studies, I turned my focus back to school and my coding journey ended there... or so I thought.

Over the next eight years, I worked for an airline and traveled all over the world taking pictures. Naturally, I moved on to videography shooting weddings and promo videos for businesses. As you can probably tell by now, I am driven by relentless curiosity, an insatiable thrist for knowledge and the thrill of a good challenge. Mastering highly technical subjects and solving complex problems are my thing. Software development seemed like a perfect fit for me. While aviation will always have a place near to my heart, a career in software development provides me with the ability to directly impact lives while challenging myself. I am extremely excited to land my first job as a developer and complete my transition!

These days, I live in Baltimore with my cat, Tituba, and dog, Kesh. In my freetime, I enjoy cooking, antiquing, golfing, traveling and flying my drone.
